The Brick

Ubiquitous and yet invisible, the clay brick is arguably one of the most relevant inventions of humankind.

Colour

Three minerals provide the base colour to the brick:

  1. Iron oxide produces the typical red colour.
  2. High levels on lime in the clay gives a yellow colour.
  3. Manganese oxide gives a brown colour.

The grey/green tones are achieved by varying the air supply in the kindling process.

1.5 Billion

The estimate global production of fired bricks is of 1.5 billion per year.
Of these, 87% are produced in Asia, 66.67% in China and 13.3% in India.
It is estimated that the brick production industry employs around 15 million people in India alone.

Uses

There are endless uses for bricks, from building walls and foundations to chimneys and fireplaces, porches, arches, architraves and terraces, outdoor steps, walks and swimming pools.
